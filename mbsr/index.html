MBSR Checklist

Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (or MBSR) is an 8-week program of meditation, yoga, and similar practices that use mindfulness to manage and reduce stress. Jon Kabat-Zinn developed MBSR starting in the 1970s, and wrote Full Catastrophe Living (1990) to detail the program and what it can do, so that anyone can complete a version of the program at home, without needing the trained guidance of the Stress Reduction Clinic.

In 2025 I read Full Catastrophe Living, and completed the MBSR program through it. This was a great experience I would recommend to anyone.

However, the 600 page book is a very detailed guide to MBSR, and I found it difficult to remember what steps were recommended at what times, so I wrote a checklist of all MBSR steps week-by-week, so I could easily check off each action and see exactly what is left to do.

That checklist became a packet, and that packet is what you can download and print here.

How to use the packet

  1. Read Full Catastrophe Living Part 1: "The Practice of Mindfulness," which explains how to perform the different practices of the program, mainly: sitting meditation, body-scan, and yoga. A checklist can't replace this. Maybe someday I could expand the packet to stand alone, but Kabat-Zinn's explanations are excellent and would be difficult to match. This section is 160 pages and feels like only 100. FCL is also available as an audiobook read by the author.

  2. Notice that many steps of MBSR are "bring mindfulness to ___." I've abbreviated this phrase to "BMT." Thus “BMT one meal this week” means “bring mindfulness to one meal this week.”

  3. Print the packet. Print it 2-sided, flipped on the short edge. Then staple the top left corner. When you open it, you'll see "Week 1 Formal" on your left and "Week 1 Informal" on your right. If you turn the page you should see "Week 2 Formal" and "Week 2 Informal". If it doesn't look like that, try printing it flipped on the "long edge." If you can't print 2-sided, just print 1-sided, and it will be slightly inconvenient to flip back and forth every week.

  4. Begin.

Extras: reading schedule

Reading the rest of Full Catastrophe Living is not important to MBSR, however, some of the book, especially the last part, are excellent. In case you'd like to read it, I've included a suggested reading schedule for reading the full book throughout the 8 weeks of MBSR. If you find this idea daunting, please skip it: without exaggeration, MBSR is 100 times more valuable than reading the other 3 sections of the book. FCL is also available as an audiobook read by the author.

Exact sources

I used the “Revised and Updated” 2013 edition of Full Catastrophe Living.

Throughout the book, descriptions of MBSR are slightly different. For example, in Chapter 10, the pleasant/unpleasant events calendars belong with Weeks 3 and 4, while in Chapter 35 these are put in Weeks 2 and 3. It seems that Kabat-Zinn intends Chapters 34 and 35 to specify MBSR’s exact program, while Chapter 10 is only a summary. Therefore I’ve made this packet exactly accurate to Chapters 34 and 35, except for these additions, which are described only in Chapter 10, but I decided to include:

  • In Week 1 informal, add “BMT one routine activity per day”

  • In Week 4 formal, formal sit may go up to 30min, not just 20min

  • In Weeks 5 and 6 formal, “Guided Sit” is an additional backup option for Yoga
